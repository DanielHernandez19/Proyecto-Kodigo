Volcanos and Parks.

Conchagua Volcano

Place: Conchagua: La Unión

Privileged views of Honduras, Nicaragua, and the Gulf of Fonseca make the Conchagua Volcano a unique experience. If you visit this destination, you will enjoy the beautiful maritime landscapes of the gulf and its islands.

This colossus is in the municipality of Conchagua, in the extreme west of the Gulf of Fonseca, in La Unión. It has two prominent hills: Cerro Ocotal and Banderas.

For the more adventurous, camping on the volcano and watching the sunrises and sunsets are unforgettable moments given that offers places full of great natural wealth and a diversity of colors. To climb the volcano, you can choose to walk up and enjoy its pleasant climate and impressive views; or ride in the vehicles that offer their services in the area.

Surf City Walter Thilo Deininger Adventure Park

Place: La Libertad

An incredible adventure in the middle of nature awaits you in this park that is part of Surf City El Salvador. For daring tourists, adrenaline, and extreme sports lovers, this place has a variety of activities such as mountain biking, rappel, and a zip line that tests your physical abilities.

And visitors who look for a place to relax and enjoy tranquility, there is the option of camping and hiking amid its natural wealth that expands over 1,047 hectares of land. Other tourist attractions for those who enjoy being surrounded by nature are the guided tours to the "El Mirador" trail where tourists can enjoy a panoramic view of the Pacific Ocean.

Santa Ana Volcano - Ilamatepec

Place: Santa Ana

Put your endurance to the test by climbing to the top of the Ilamatepec volcano, also called the Santa Ana volcano. If you make it, you are sure to have some of the most impressive views of the area, with the turquoise waters of Lake Coatepeque as a background.

Located in the department of Santa Ana, this colossus has an altitude of 2,381 meters above sea level, the highest in El Salvador. Its last eruptions occurred in 2005.

This volcano is part of the Los Volcanes Complex in the Apaneca mountain range, within a tropical mountainous cloud forest, in a coffee-growing region about 65 kilometers west of the capital city. The main activities in the region are agriculture and tourism so you will be rewarded by the luscious landscapes that you will see from the moment you start your ride along the road, lined with fruit stalls that characterize the area.

Coatepeque Lake

Place: Santa Ana

Its blue waters and high ecotourism offer make Lake Coatepeque one of the favorite destinations for Salvadorans and foreigners, here you can practice kayaking, jet skiing, and diving.

This volcanic lake has abundant natural wealth. Visitors can observe a great variety of resident and migratory birds or go fishing, diving or riding a kayak, jet skiing, or boat riding. In addition, the surroundings entice you to go hiking or mountain biking, relish the beautiful panoramic views covered with lush vegetation.

Its waters change from blue to turquoise green. According to authorities of the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources of El Salvador, this is a cyclical phenomenon that occurs from time to time. The color change has happened in 1998, 2006, 2012, 2016, 2017, 2018, and 2019.

Costa del Sol Water Park

Place: San Luis La Herradura: La Paz

The main attraction of this park is that you can live two different experiences in one place: Taking a walk through the mangrove swamp of the Estero de Jaltepeque or taking a swim in the Costa del Sol beach.

This destination is located in San Luis La Herradura in the department of La Paz. Offering one of the most beautiful beaches with the best sunsets in El Salvador. It also has swimming pools, cabins, restaurants, a basketball court, and has one of the largest beach soccer stadiums in the country, where international sporting events take place.

Mercado del Mar (Sea Market)

Place: La Libertad

The Mercado del Mar offers visitors a place to shop or taste fresh seafood that will surely be one of the best gastronomic experiences of your life. Located just 40 minutes away from the capital city. The market has 20 restaurants that await you with special seafood dishes and a la carte dining. And the new Surf City Road has made traffic a jam of the past, making it easier to enjoy a family trip.

This beachside destination offers visitors refreshing “minutas” (Scraped ice with fruit juices) and coconuts, handicrafts, and traditional "antojitos" (snacks) plus you'll be able to see first-hand all the variety of fresh seafood and buy those of your choice. Discover this destination with your family and friends, enjoying a good rest, tasty food, music, and the sea breeze that garnishes this place.
