Towns.

San Ignacio

Place: Chalatenango

If yours is eco and agrotourism, you have to visit this natural destination, rich in vegetation of pines, cypresses and all kinds of vegetables, and fauna, especially tepezcuintles, squirrels, agoutis, deer, raccoons, margays and others.

San Ignacio is the starting point for you to start your adventure to various attractions such as: fruit and vegetable cooperatives, El Pital hill and Peñón de Cayaguanca and other destinations that will captivate you.

This destination is for adventurers, suitable for hiking, camping, mountaineering and many other activities where you will enjoy scenic beauties, forests, flora and fauna; and you can also choose to savor dishes and products from the area.

La Palma

Place: Chalatenango

The most representative handicrafts of El Salvador are made in this place, a colorful municipality surrounded by pine trees that create an atmosphere of pure air that you will love. In this destination you can find most of its houses painted with naive designs, very typical of the place that give color to this place. This technique was created by Fernando Llort, one of the most recognized Salvadoran artists nationally and internationally.

During your visit, it is essential that you visit the workshops where handicrafts are made in wood, leather, ceramics, seeds and cotton clothing. As part of the experience that the destination offers, you will be able to put your painting skills to the test. Being a town with many attractions, you can hire a guide to take you through the most emblematic sites, such as the craft market, the church, among others. One of the most popular guided tours is the historical one in which you will learn where the first dialogue for peace was held and the clock tower, which dates back to 1932.

Concepcion de Ataco

Place: Ahuachapan

One of the most colorful destinations in El Salvador awaits you! With its cobbled streets, colorful murals, handicrafts, gastronomy and a cool climate, this is Ataco, another destination on the Ruta de Las Flores.

At 1,260 meters above sea level, surrounded by imposing hills full of vegetation and prominent coffee plantations, in Ataco you can enjoy a greater ecotourism experience, since it has spectacular gardens, thermal water pools, and waterfalls.

If you are a coffee lover, you will surely like the Coffee Tour, an activity that you can do in the surrounding farms. You can also visit the artisan looms, go to the viewpoint of Cerro de La Cruz, visit the plastic arts workshops, walk its streets to observe the colorful murals and visit the church.

Juayúa

Lugar: Sonsonate

In the middle of mountains and spectacular landscapes is Juayúa, another municipality on the Ruta de Las Flores, awaits you with a cool climate, full of landscapes and natural destinations in Sonsonate, in western El Salvador.

Las Siete Cascadas, where you can climb or rappel in more than one; Las Ranas Lagoon, which is located in the Protected Natural Area of the same name; and Portezuelo Park, an ecological park in the middle of a cloudy forest are destinations that you can enjoy in Juayúa.

You can also take tours of the city on horseback, in carriages or by train. In the urban area you will find craft shops, cafes, nurseries, restaurants, art galleries and on weekends you can taste different dishes at its famous gastronomic festival.

Apaneca

Place: Ahuachapán

The cold climate of Apaneca is compensated with a cup of coffee of the highest quality, harvested in the area. Apaneca is one of the destinations that make up the Ruta de Las Flores, it is located in the department of Ahuachapán at 1,477 meters above sea level, an ideal place for lovers of cool weather.

If ecotourism is your thing, Apaneca has destinations such as Laguna Verde and Laguna Las Ninfas, both of which are ideal for hiking and exploring the flora and fauna of the area. In the destinations you can also do canopy, mountain biking and buggy rides, since the area has several destinations that invite you to test your skills.

The area also has curious and mystical places such as the Albanian Labyrinth, a huge ecological labyrinth that challenges you to find the exit; and Hoyo de Cuajusto, an extinct volcanic crater that preserves some caves. Do not worry about accommodation, in the area there is a cozy hostel at an affordable price, you can also choose between cabins or more rustic hotels.

Antiguo Cuscatlan

Place: La Libertad

Despite being a relatively new city, Antiguo Cuscatlán has become a highly visited tourist destination due to its boom in modern infrastructure through imposing commercial and vertical high-value residential centers. If you like shopping, here you will be able to appreciate modern shopping centers, which due to their size and renowned stores are a real attraction for tourists. In addition, they have a semi-open infrastructure that allows you to have a pleasant time while you do your shopping.

Highly recognized hotels are also housed in this municipality, a city recommended for express or business trips. In addition, there are small local shops selling traditional Salvadoran food that will allow you to enjoy delicious dishes such as pupusa and others made from corn.
